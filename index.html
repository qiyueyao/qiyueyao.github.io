Adelaide

South Australia

Adelaide is the capital city of the state of South Australia and the fifth-most populous city of Australia.(wiki) It ranks high in terms of liveability. The city center is small but delicate, surrounded by parks. Relaxing is easy in Adelaide. Just hop on a tram and go to Glenelg. I took the photo at the beach in a sunny afternoon.

Chongqing

Chongqing

Southwest China

Chongqing is one of the four direct-controlled municipalities in China. Only using two keywords to categorize the city would be "spicy" and "mountain" Due to the dry climate there, people in Chongqing especially like hot and spicy food. Hot pot is one of the must tries! And due to geography, Chongqing is a maze built on the mountains, too magical to find ways. In the photo is the cable way crossing the Yangtze River.

Rotterdam

Rotterdam

South Holland

Rotterdam is the second largest city in Netherlands, just behind Amsterdam, but way more different. It's the largest port in Europe. Rotterdam has evolved from a dam in 1270 to a very industrialized city nowadays. Looking for strange and interesting buildings and Rotterdam should fit. The photo was taken in an early morning beside the Waalhaven.

Hangzhou

East China

Hangzhou is the capital of Zhejiang Province. It's a city with long history and a city where you feel typical Jiangnan aroma, especially around the West Lake. It's the most popular tourist attraction and located right at the city center, but surrounded by hills. A best place for an after-work walk and that makes Hangzhou even more elegant.

Qinghai

Qinghai

Northwest China

Qinghai is the fourth largest province in size, with average elevation over 3,000 metres (9,800 ft) above sea level. It's located on the northeastern part of the Tibetan Plateau. The photo shows Qinghai Lake, largest lake (saline and alkaline) in China. Most popular animal? Yak.

Whitsunday

Whitsunday

Queensland Australia

The Whitsunday Islands is a collection of continental islands of various sizes off the central coast of Queensland, Australia, approximately 900 kilometres (560 miles) north of Brisbane.(wiki) It's most famous for the heart-shaped reef "hidden" among the Great Berrier Reef. I took the photo on a helicopter on the way to the reef. Hamiloton Island, the largest in Whitsunday Islands has pure white sand beaches.
